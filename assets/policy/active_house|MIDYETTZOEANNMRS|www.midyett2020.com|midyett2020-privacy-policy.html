    PRIVACY POLICY
     

    ​Zoe Midyett for US Congress (the Campaign) has created this privacy policy to explain how we use information that you may provide while visiting the Site and to demonstrate our firm commitment to internet privacy.  The campaign may modify this policy as necessary, so we encourage you to check this page when revisiting the Site. By using the Site, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. 
     
    HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION 

    When you register, donate, sign up to volunteer, or take any other action on the Site, we may ask you to give us contact information. This information may include your name, address, telephone number, mobile telephone number, and/or e-mail address. We may obtain information about you from outside sources and combine it with the information we collect through the Site. 
     
    We may use this information to operate the Site, send you news and information about the Campaign’s activities, solicit your participation in the Campaign’s activities, events, and programs, and for related uses. We may use your e-mail address to send such information by e-mail, and use your telephone number to call you or send you a text message for these purposes. 
     
    Additionally, we may share your information as follows, or as otherwise described in this Privacy Policy.
     
    To companies that assist us in maintaining the Site or with the Campaign activities, but only for purposes of providing services to the Campaign, and with the strict requirement that any such company will never itself have the right to contact you for any reason.
     
    To comply with applicable law or legal requirements (for example, a lawful subpoena), to protect our rights or property, or to protect our supporters from fraudulent, abusive, or unlawful conduct, or if we reasonably believe that an emergency involving immediate danger of death or serious physical injury to any person requires disclosure of communications or justifies disclosure of records; to other groups with your consent. 
     
    Submitting your personal information, as described above, on this page will sign you up to receive e-mails from the Campaign. We may also use your first name to indicate the latest actions taken on this site. 
     
    CHILDREN 
     
    The Campaign complies with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act. We do not solicit and do not accept personally identifying information from any person under 13 years of age. 
     
    Visitors to this Site who are under 13 years of age should not use the Site except under the supervision and with the assistance of their parent or legal guardian and should never submit any personally identifying information to the Site. Visitors under 13 years of age should not register, purchase, or use services or take any actions on or through the Site. 
     
    CREDIT CARD INFORMATION - PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION 
     
    When you contribute or purchase services/goods through the Site, we collect credit card information from you. That information is used solely for processing your contribution or purchase of services/goods. 
     
    The Campaign uses industry standard security measures to protect against the loss, misuse, or alteration of all of the personally identifiable information that you provide to us. Our server is located in a secure environment. All supplied credit card information is transmitted via PayPal. See PayPal’s privacy policy, legal agreements, and policy updates.
     
    Permission to access your personally identifiable information is granted only to you and the Campaign employees or contractors who need to know that information to provide services to you, and they are required to keep this information strictly confidential. Although we make good faith efforts to store information collected by the Site in a secure operating environment, we cannot guarantee complete security. 
     
    CONTRIBUTIONS TO THE CAMPAIGN – PUBLIC INFORMATION
     
    The Campaign is required to file regular reports with the Federal Election Commission that publicly disclose the name, address, occupation, and employer of persons who contribute over $200 during an election cycle, along with the amount and date of the contribution. 
     
    COOKIES AND DATA TRACKING 
     
    A cookie is a piece of data stored on the user’s hard drive containing information about the user. Some parts of the Site may be password-protected. We may use cookies. Cookies are stored on your computer and are controlled by your browser. The use of cookies helps us remember and process items that you purchase through the Site, and/or to compile aggregate data about visitors to the Site and their interaction with the Site for the purposes of improving the operation of the Site, and/or offering better Site experiences and tools in the future. 
     
    We may also use cookies to enable you to return to password-protected areas of the Site without having to re-enter your password.  If you wish to disable these cookies, you may do so from your browser. However, disabling the use of cookies may not allow the Site to work normally. Disabling the use of cookies may also prevent you from accessing portions of the Site. 
     
    We may also use third-party services such as Google Analytics in order to analyze traffic patterns as well as discover problems within the Site. We may also use embedded images in emails to track opening rates for our mailings, so that we can tell which mailings appeal most to Campaign supporters. 
     
    ADVERTISING 
     
    We may advertise with third-party vendors, including Google, which will be shown on other Sites on the internet. In some cases, those third-party vendors may decide which ads to show you based on your prior visits to the Site. At no time will you be personally identified to those third-party vendors, nor will any of the information you share with us be shared with those third-party vendors.  If you prefer to opt out of the use of these third-party cookies on the Site, you can do so. 
     
    The Campaign does not use cookies to track what specific pages an individual visits. Please note, however, that third party vendors, including Google, may show the Campaign-sponsored ads on the Internet. Those third-party vendors (including Google) use cookies to serve ads based on a user’s prior visits to our website. Users may opt out of Google’s use of cookies by clicking here.

     

    LINKS TO OTHER SITES 
     
    The privacy policies and practices contained in this Privacy Policy do not apply to any third-party websites linked to by the Site. This Privacy Policy only applies to our Site or any future webpages that we may develop. It does not cover third-party websites that are linked to by the Site or websites for which we are not responsible (linked Sites). These linked Sites will have their own policies and practices that may be different from ours. We encourage you to familiarize yourself with the policies and practices of the linked Sites, especially if you provide personal information to them. 
     
    CALIFORNIA ONLINE PRIVACY PROTECTION ACT COMPLIANCE 
     
    The Campaign complies with the California Online Privacy Protection Act. As part of that Act, all users of our Site may make any changes to their information at any time by contacting us as described below. Changes to billing and/or credit card information must be made through the Campaign staff. Please contact us as described below with any questions. 
     
    UNSUBSCRIBE OR OPT OUT 
     
    You can opt out of receiving future information via email by clicking here. 
     
    AMENDMENTS AND CONSENT TO THIS PRIVACY POLICY 
     
    We retain the right to amend or otherwise update this Privacy Policy at any time. By using our Site, you consent to the collection and use of the information as we have described. If we change our policies and practices, we will post the changes in our Privacy Policy so that you may become aware of them. With this knowledge, you can make an informed decision about whether you wish to provide personal information to us. 
     
    HOW TO CONTACT US 
     
    If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, you may contact us by clicking here. The Campaign generally responds within 24 hours.
