Joe Wilson for Congress Online Privacy Policy

Welcome to joemeansjobs.com, the official website of Joe Wilson’s campaign for Congress. This Online Privacy Policy (this “Policy”) describes how we collect, use and protect visitors’ personal and non-personal information provided to us on joemeansjobs.com.

Personal Information

The only personal information we collect on joewilsonforcongress.com is information that you provide. “Personal information,” as used in this Policy, means information that is directly associated with a particular person, such as name, address, telephone number and email address. We may request personal information in order to provide various types of services or information to you. For example, personal information may be requested in connection with your affirmative request to receive email or other communications from us, a contribution made to Joe Wilson for Congress. You agree that by providing your phone number you are providing an electronic signature and you expressly authorize Joe Wilson for Congress to contact you at any email address shown above for purposes of conveying information about the cause.

Our general policy is to not make personal information provided online available to anyone other than our employees, staff and agents, except in the limited circumstances described in this Policy. We will not rent or sell your personal information to third parties. We may share your personal information with like-minded organizations to complement the activities of Joe Wilson for Congress.

Third parties providing services to us may have limited access to your personal information. Access to your personal information by these service providers is limited to the information reasonably necessary in order for them to perform their limited function for us. We require these service providers to protect the privacy of your personal information in a manner consistent with this Policy, and to not use or disclose your personal information for any purpose other than providing the service to us.

We may release personal information to third parties to comply with valid legal requirements such as a law, regulation, search warrant, subpoena or court order. Additionally, we may disclose personal information where we believe doing so is reasonably necessary to investigate or prevent harm, fraud, abuse or illegal conduct, or to protect the safety and security of visitors to joewilsonforcongress.com.

The security of your personal information is important to us. We maintain a variety of physical, electronic and administrative safeguards to protect personal information you provide to us. For example, we have adopted commercially reasonable tools and techniques to protect against unauthorized access to our systems. We use a secured/encrypted server for online credit card contributions and online store transactions on joewilsonforcongress.com.

Non-Personally Identifiable Information

We automatically collect certain types of non-personally identifiable information from your use of joewilsonforcongress.com. For example, we log IP addresses, which are the locations of computers on the Internet, and collect information about the aggregate number of visits to each page within joewilsonforcongress.com. We use this non-personally identifiable information to track the popularity of certain pages and to diagnose and resolve any problems with the website.

Cookies

We make use of “cookies,” which are small files sent to your computer’s hard drive, to personalize and customize your interaction with joewilsonforcongress.com. The information collected by cookies helps us generate content responsive to your interests and also allows us to collect statistics about how many people visit joewilsonforcongress.com and for what purpose. Our cookies do not contain any personal information about you, and we do not use them to collect or request any personal information. You may be able to modify your Internet browser settings to refuse cookies. Disabling cookies may limit our ability to customize joewilsonforcongress.com for you and to deliver a better user experience to you.

Public Information

Any information you post on blogs, discussion boards or other public areas of joewilsonforcongress.com and associated social media sites will be disclosed to the public by virtue of your posting and thus is not governed by this Policy. We do not control the use and disclosure of this information. Information that you post to a public area or communicate through a public area of joewilsonforcongress.com may be used by third parties to contact you or to communicate this information to others, including in other public areas of other web sites. Please do not post any information, including personal information, in a public area of joewilsonforcongress.com that you do not want disclosed to the general public.

When you provide comments to us, we treat your name, city, state, and any comments you provide as public information. We may, for example, provide compilations of your comments to our leaders and other individuals participating in our efforts, without disclosing email addresses. We may also make comments along with your city and state available to the press and public online.

Children

The safety and privacy of children online is important to us. We request that children under 18 not submit their information on joemeansjobs.com. Further, in accordance with the Federal Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 (COPPA), we do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from persons under thirteen years of age on joewilsonforcongress.com.

How to unsubscribe

Should you wish to opt-out of communications from joemeansjobs.com, an unsubscribe link is included in every email. If the link is not working properly or you wish to communicate your desire to opt-out immediately, send an email with “Unsubscribe” in the subject to info@teamjoewilson.com.

Changes to this Online Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to revise or update this Policy at any time. If we make any changes to this Policy, we will post the updated Policy and will update the date at the bottom of the page.
