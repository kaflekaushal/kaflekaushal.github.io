Cookies

YourMembership may place “cookies” on the browser of your computer. Cookies are small pieces of information that are stored by your browser on your computer’s hard drive. Cookies may enhance the convenience and use of the Website. For example, the information provided through cookies may be used to recognize you as a previous user of the Website (so you do not have to enter your personal information every time), offer personalized information for your use, and otherwise facilitate your experience using the Website. You may choose to decline cookies if your browser permits, but doing so may affect your ability to access or use certain features of the Website.

There are four categories of cookies that YourMembership utilizes: “Strictly Necessary,” “Performance,” “Functionality,” and “Third-Party”. These cookie categories are detailed below.

  • • Strictly Necessary Cookies. These cookies are essential for the ability to access and use the features of our Websites such as accessing secure areas. These cookies include your login credentials and session ID.
  • • Performance Cookies. These cookies are used to measure and improve Website performance by collecting information on how visitors use our Websites. This information includes your location, how you accessed the Website, how long you spent there, and which actions you took. All information collected by these cookies is aggregated and cannot be used to identify visitors.
  • • Functionality Cookies. These cookies are used to remember preferences and choices you make while using our Websites. These cookies are typically triggered by a user action and are used to provide a better overall experience when visiting our Websites but are not necessary for our Websites to work. For example, we use a cookie to display a job alert sign up form on search results pages.
  • • Third-Party Cookies. These cookies may be placed on your browser on behalf of our Partners or other third-party service providers. These cookies can be used to display advertisements, build a user profile based on your browsing activity, and measure the performance of advertising campaigns, but are not linked to site functionality on our Websites. Information collected from these cookies may be shared with other organizations besides YourMembership such as advertising networks.

These cookies will remain on your computer for a length of time dependent on whether it is a persistent or session cookie. Persistent cookies remain on your computer until they expire or are deleted manually. Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close or restart your browser.

YourMembership may also utilize web beacons on our Websites. A “web beacon,” also known as an Internet tag, pixel tag or clear GIF, links web pages to web servers and their cookies and may be used to transmit information collected through cookies back to a web server. The information collected through these automated means may include information that we gather and store about your visit to, and use of, the Websites, such as your IP address, type of browser and operating system used, date and time you access the websites, pages you visit, and, if you linked to the Websites from another website, the address of that website. This data does not include Personal Information and is not treated as Personal Information under this Privacy Policy, but it may include the IP address of your device. We may link an IP address to information that is personally identifiable.

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can disable this function so that your browser will not accept cookies. Please be aware that disabling this function may impact your use and enjoyment of this Website.

Do Not Track

Currently, various browsers — including Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, and Apple Safari — offer a “do not track” or “DNT” option that relies on a technology known as a DNT header, which sends a signal to Web sites’ visited by the user about the user's browser DNT preference setting. We do not currently commit to responding to browsers' DNT signals with respect to the Company's Web sites, in part, because no common industry standard for DNT has been adopted by industry groups, technology companies or regulators, including no consistent standard of interpreting user intent. We take privacy and meaningful choice seriously and will make efforts to continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and the implementation of a standard.
