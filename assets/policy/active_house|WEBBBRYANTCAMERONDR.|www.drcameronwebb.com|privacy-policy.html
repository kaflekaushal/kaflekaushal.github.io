    Privacy Policy

    We care about protecting the privacy and security of the information you share with us. We have created this privacy policy to explain our practices on this website. Our site may link to third party websites, for which we are not responsible. We may revise this privacy policy as appropriate due to new practices or technologies, and will update this page accordingly.

     

    Information Collected:

     

    We collect information about those who register on our site, make contributions through our site, or take other actions on the site. Personal information is information that can be directly associated with a specific person or entity such as a name, address, telephone number, credit card information, bank account information, email address, or information about activities that are directly linked to that person.

     

    Automatic Information Collection:

     

    We automatically receive certain types of information when you interact with us online. Information may be collected through the use of “cookies” or tracking pixels. This information may include your IP address, web browser, or interaction with our email campaigns or advertisements.

     

    Information on Children:

     

    We do not knowingly collect information of any kind from children under the age of 13, in accordance with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act. Children under the age of 13 should not provide any personal information on this site.

     

    Using Your Information:

     

    We will use your email address to provide you news and information about our activities. We may also use location information to send you email messages about upcoming events or activities in your area.

     

    When you sign one of our petitions, we treat your name, city, state, and any comments as public information. We may, for example, disclose this information to public officials, the press, and/or the general public.

     

    We may be required to disclose information regarding our contributions to comply with campaign finance laws. For example, federal law requires us to use our best efforts to collect and report the name, mailing address, occupation and name of employer of individuals whose contributions aggregate in excess of $200 in a calendar year.

     

    We may disclose your contact information to other organizations or campaigns with similar goals that we believe you may wish to hear from.

     

    We will not sell or exchange your credit card information to any other third party under any circumstances.

     

    Unsubscribing from Email Communications:

     

    You may unsubscribe from our email communications at any time. We will regularly process these requests. To comply with the law, however, our contributor records will not be altered and are continuously maintained in a separate secure database.
